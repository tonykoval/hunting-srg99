Appendix C Bibliography

  1. J. H. Conway, Five $\$1{,}000$ Problems (Problem 1: the $99$-graph), Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences / Conway's problem list, 2017.
  2. A. E. Brouwer, Parameters of Strongly Regular Graphs (online table), www.win.tue.nl/~aeb/graphs/srg/. The "$?$" entry for $(99,14,1,2)$.
  3. A. E. Brouwer, H. Van Maldeghem, Strongly Regular Graphs, Cambridge University Press, 2022.
  4. A. E. Brouwer, W. H. Haemers, Spectra of Graphs, Springer, 2012.
  5. E. Cesarz, A. J. Woldar, On automorphisms of a putative $\mathrm{SRG}(99,14,1,2)$, 2023 — establishes the unique order-$7$ configuration $[1,7^{14}]$ (Lemma 2.2).
  6. A. Keramatipour, Approaching the Conway-99 problem using SAT solvers, MPhil thesis, University of Cambridge, 2023 (arXiv:2604.23037) — the SAT verdict and the claw-minimisation reformulation of Chapter 4.
  7. M. Behbahani, C. Lam, Strongly regular graphs with non-trivial automorphisms, Discrete Math. 311 (2011) 132–144 — the orbit-matrix method of Chapter 3.
  8. S. Shpectorov, X. Zhao, Strongly regular graphs with parameters $(85,14,3,2)$ do not exist, 2025 (arXiv:2504.02449) — the Euclidean / triangle-Gram method whose non-engagement on Conway-99 is the subject of Chapter 5.
  9. The sister volume, Hunting SRG(37) (github.com/tonykoval/hunting-srg37), and Hunting SRG(69) (github.com/tonykoval/hunting-srg69).
  10. Companion search engine: github.com/tonykoval/orbit-gen — the orbit-matrix enumerator, SMS solver, Gram-PSD filter and SAT encodings used throughout.
