A.2   Glossary

Conway 99-graph. An $\mathrm{SRG}(99,14,1,2)$: $99$ vertices, every edge in a unique triangle ($\lambda=1$), every non-edge in a unique quadrilateral ($\mu=2$). The subject of one of Conway's five $\$1000$ problems.

Open existence question. Parameters that pass every feasibility test but admit no known graph and no non-existence proof. $(99,14,1,2)$ is the famous example.

Orbit matrix. The quotient of the adjacency matrix by a prescribed automorphism (Behbahani–Lam); zero orbit matrices ⇒ no graph with that symmetry, a liftable one ⇒ a graph.

Semiregular automorphism. One with no fixed points (all orbits of size $p$); possible only for $p\mid 99$, i.e. $p\in\{3,11\}$.

Cesarz–Woldar configuration. The unique order-$7$ action on $\mathrm{SRG}(99,14,1,2)$: one fixed point and $14$ orbits of size $7$, written $[1,7^{14}]$. Existence within it is open.

Cayley graph / difference set. A graph $\mathrm{Cay} (G,S)$; an SRG iff $S$ is a partial difference set. Neither abelian group of order $99$ carries one.

Euclidean / Gram embedding. Vertices map to the $(-4)$-eigenspace ($\dim 44$) as unit vectors; the Gram matrix is PSD of rank $44$. A partial graph whose forced Gram matrix is not PSD of rank $\le 44$ cannot extend.

Amenability margin. $(3k-3\lambda)-\min(f,g)$; the triangle Gram filter over-constrains a graph only when this is positive. For Conway-99 it is $-5$ — the filter does not engage.

Claw-minimisation. Recasting existence as: minimise the number of neighbourhood claws over $14$-regular, $\mu=2$ graphs; zero is attainable iff the SRG exists (Theorem B.6).

The iron law (of this series). A strongly regular graph that is both large and nearly symmetry-free forces full enumeration cost: symmetry-based methods reach only symmetric graphs, and raw search drowns at low symmetry. It is the common reason all three Hunting SRG targets resist every general method.
