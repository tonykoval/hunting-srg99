An Interactive Hunt Log Hunting SRG(99)

John Conway offered $\$1000$ for it. Does a strongly regular graph $\mathrm{SRG}(99,14,1,2)$ exist — a graph on $99$ vertices in which every edge lies in a unique triangle and every non-edge in a unique quadrilateral? A decade on, nobody has collected. This book is the map of the hunt.

The one-sentence problem

A graph on $99$ vertices, $14$-regular, with $\lambda=1$ (adjacent vertices share one common neighbour) and $\mu=2$ (non-adjacent share two). The parameters pass every feasibility test ever devised, yet no such graph has been built and none proved impossible.

The motivating question

Conway's "$99$-graph problem" is one of his five $\$1000$ problems. A neighbour, $\mathrm{SRG}(85,14,3,2)$, was refuted by Shpectorov–Zhao (2025); Conway's remains the most famous open door in the strongly-regular tables. Build one graph and win the prize; rule out every possibility and refute it. Either ending is a result.

What this book is

An honest, reproducible hunt log on three fronts — reduce by symmetry, encode as logic, constrain by geometry — and a precise account of how far each reaches. The symmetric cases are falling order by order; the SAT and geometric attacks have produced sharp negative findings; and what remains is a short list of famous open doors.

Already proven

$14$-regularity is forced; every neighbourhood is seven disjoint edges; no graph has an automorphism of order $11$ or $3$; none is an abelian Cayley graph; and — uniquely among open cases — the triangle-Gram filter that settled $(85,14,3,2)$ does not engage here (Appendix B). What is left is the order-$7$ door $[1,7^{14}]$, the involutions, and the rigid core.

One question, three shapes

This is one of three linked hunt logs. The same object — a strongly regular graph — poses three genuinely different questions, and the same wall stops every general method on each: a target that is large and nearly symmetry-free forces full enumeration cost. That common lesson, met from three angles, is the spine of the series.

The Hunting SRG series
  • SRG(37,18,8,9) — completeness: is the catalog of $6802$ conference graphs final, or is there a $6803$rd?
  • SRG(69,20,7,5) — existence: the smallest parameters with no known graph and no proof.
  • SRG(99,14,1,2) — existence, famous: Conway's $\$1000$ graph.  (you are here)

Map of the book

Part I — The Problem. Conway's question and why it resists (Ch 1); the forced degree, the $7K_2$ neighbourhood, the spectrum $\{14,3^{54},(-4)^{44}\}$, the $231$ triangles (Ch 2).

Part II — The Hunt. The automorphism sweep and the order-$7$ door (Ch 3); the SAT verdict and the claw-minimisation reformulation (Ch 4); the Euclidean / Gram attack and its unique non-engagement (Ch 5).

Part III — Frontier. What is proven, the three walls and their single cause, the open doors, and the decision procedure (Ch 6); and the exact commands that reproduce every closure and drive the open doors (Ch 7).
