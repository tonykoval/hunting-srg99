Chapter 5 · Part II Spectral & Geometric Attacks

The method that recently refuted a neighbouring open case, $\mathrm{SRG}(85,14,3,2)$, is geometric: embed the graph in Euclidean space and over-constrain the local triangles. Run the same machinery on Conway's graph and something striking happens — it is the one case where the triangle filter does not engage. Understanding why is the sharpest structural fact in this book.

5.1   The Euclidean embedding

Map each vertex to a unit vector in the $s=-4$ eigenspace, of dimension $g=44$. Adjacency fixes the inner products (one value for edges, another for non-edges), so the would-be graph is a configuration of $99$ unit vectors whose Gram matrix $G$ is positive semidefinite of rank exactly $44$. Any partial graph whose forced Gram matrix fails to be PSD of rank $\le 44$ cannot extend — a sound, symmetry-free pruning oracle, and the engine behind the Shpectorov–Zhao style of non-existence proof.

5.2   Why the triangle filter bites — usually

The decisive sharpening is local. Around each triangle, the Gram constraints on the $3k-3\lambda+t$ vectors of its neighbourhood are over-determined when that count exceeds the global multiplicity cap $\min(f,g)$ — the Gram matrix simply cannot be realised, and the case collapses. This is exactly what kills $(85,14,3,2)$: its triangle neighbourhood overflows the cap, the local PSD system is infeasible, and non-existence follows.

5.3   Conway's graph uniquely escapes

The non-engagement, in numbers

For $(99,14,1,2)$ the triangle-neighbourhood count is $3k-3\lambda+t = 42-3+t = 39+t$, while the multiplicity cap is $\min(f,g)=\min(54,44)=44$. The "amenability" margin is $39 - 44 = -5 < 0$: the local system is under-determined, so the triangle filter cannot over-constrain it. Conway's graph is the one open case where $\lambda=1$ rigidifies the neighbourhood locally yet the large multiplicity $44$ of $s=-4$ leaves the geometry too much room. The folklore that "$\lambda=1$ makes a graph tractable" is, for Conway-99, simply false.

This is a real, if negative, finding: the very tool that settled the neighbour $(85,14,3,2)$ provably does not reach Conway's graph at the triangle level. The local rigidity that should help instead arrives too small to bite.

5.4   The triangle reformulation

A complementary view moves to the $231$ triangles of Chapter 2. Existence of $\mathrm{SRG}(99,14,1,2)$ is equivalent to a regular structure on those triangles with parameters $(231,18,5,\le 3)$ — a sparser object ($k/n = 18/231\approx 0.08$) whose own Gram and incidence constraints can be propagated independently. It is the encoding our tractability heuristic favours, but it is a separate, large search in its own right.

What the geometry leaves

The Euclidean / Gram filter is sound and runs, but on Conway-99 it neither over-constrains the local triangles nor exhausts the global configuration on one machine — like the order-$7$ door and the SAT encoding, it is cluster-scale. The forced rank-$44$ Gram structure (the $7K_2$ neighbourhood plus the global PSD condition) is exactly what the distributed attack of Chapter 6 propagates.
